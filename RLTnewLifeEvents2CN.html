All full-time permanent male employees after 1 year of service with the Company, excluding those who are serving their notice period, are entitled to 20 days of Family Leave (including paternity leave) on full pay for each confinement of his spouse. Eligible employees must inform their manager in advance of their intention of taking Family Leave.
A copy of birth certificate or any official documents showing the relationship between employee and the child should be submitted to the People and Development Department. The Family Leave can be taken consecutively or as a block of at least 5 work days each time and must be taken within 1 year of the child’s birth or adoption.
Any untaken day(s) will be forfeited after the 1 year period. The Family Leave is subject to the Company’s absolute discretion. It cannot be cashed out under any circumstances.

Maternity leave

All full-time female employees are entitled to Maternity Leave for 98 calendar days, starting 15 days before the expected date of confinement, subject to local statutory requirements.
For the purposes of work safety and protection under the relevant laws, female employees are required to submit a medical certificate of pregnancy issued by a registered medical practitioner within 12 weeks of pregnancy, and birth certificate & discharge summary within one month after the birth of the child.


Paternity leave

All full-time male employees are entitled Statutory Paternity Leave on full pay for each confinement of his spouse, which should can be taken consecutively during the period:

- The Paternity Leave should be taken consecutively within 3 months of the child’s birth. Any untaken day(s) will be forfeited after that and cannot be cashed out or carried over under any circumstances.

- Eligible employees shall inform the line manager and People and Development Department when applying for Paternity Leave at least one month in advance. A copy of birth certificate should be submitted within one month after the birth of the child.

A birth gift of RL products will be offered for an employee’s newborn baby.

In the case of adoption, full-time permanent employees after 1 year of service with the Company, excluding those who are serving their notice period, are entitled to 20 days of Family Leave on full pay per adoption.
A copy of birth certificate or any official documents showing the relationship between employee and the child should be submitted to the People and Development Department. The Family Leave can be taken consecutively or as a block of at least 5 work-days each time and has to be taken within 1 year of the adoption. Any untaken day(s) will be forfeited after the 1-year period.

- Serious Illness of Immediate Family -

All full-time permanent employees after 1 year of service with the Company, excluding those who are serving their notice period, are entitled to 20 days of family leave on full pay for taking care an immediate family member (spouse, child, parent or parent-in-law) who has a serious illness.
The Family Leave can be taken consecutively or intermittently and can only be granted once in a 1-year look-back period.
In situations for the caring of an immediate family member, employees will need to provide the relevant medical certification to support the need for taking the leave. The certification shall include information on the date of commencement of the serious health condition, the probable duration of the condition, the necessary duration of the leave, the estimated amount of time the health care provider believes care will be needed, a confirmation that the serious health condition warrants the employee’s participation.
Eligible employees must inform their manager in advance of their intention of taking family leave. The Family Leave is subject to the Company’s absolute discretion. It cannot be cashed out under any circumstances. For details, please contact the People and Development Department.

- Death or Hospitalization of Family Members -

Full-time employees are legally entitled to 3 days' full paid funeral leave for the death of immediate family. In terms of " immediate family ", our company refers to parents, spouses, children, parents in law (in laws), brothers, sisters and grandparents.
Employees should take funeral leave within 1 month after the death of immediate family. Any untaken day(s) will be forfeited after that and cannot be cashed out or carried over under any circumstances

The supervisor/ manager of the employee concerned may initiate the arrangements (flowers or fruit basket or wreath) for such occasions. Reimbursement will be arranged by the Finance Department.
