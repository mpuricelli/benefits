Can I take the leave before the marriage date?

Yes. Upon completion of the probationary period, full-time permanent and 1 year or above contract employees are entitled to marriage leave of 3 working days on their first legal marriage. Marriage leave can only be taken once during an employee’s employment with Ralph Lauren. The marriage leave must be taken within 6 months of the official marriage registration date and the application must be supported by a copy of the marriage certificate.

What document need to provide if I married in other country?

Within 3 months (i.e. 90 days) from marriage date, you have to update marital status (Personal -> Personal Information -> Edit) and dependent’s information (Actions -> View Dependents -> Add) in Workday. Please also provide a copy of the foreign legal documents and the dependent’s passport for verification.

When can I enroll and make the benefit changes?

Within 3 months (i.e. 90 days) from marriage date, you have to update marital status (Personal -> Personal Information -> Edit) and spouse’s (or partner’s) information (Actions -> View Dependents -> Add) in Workday. Please also provide a copy of legal marriage certificate and spouse’s (or partner’s) HKID copy for verification. Please provide passport copy is not available. After that, you will receive the email from CXA for benefit selection, please complete the selection and checked out the process by deadline (around 2 – 3 weeks). Then, Manulife will issue the medical card for your dependent, it will take around 2 weeks.

NewBorns

Can I apply Family Leave (formerly named as Parental Leave) on top of Paternity Leave?

All full-time permanent male employees after 1 year of service with the Company, excluding those who are serving their notice period, are entitled to 15 days of Family Leave on full pay for each confinement of his spouse. Eligible employees must inform their manager in advance of their intention of taking Family Leave. A copy of birth certificate or any official documents showing the relationship between employee and the child should be submitted to the People and Development Department. The Family Leave can be taken consecutively or as a block of at least 5 work days each time and must be taken within 1 year of the child’s birth or adoption. Any untaken day(s) will be forfeited after the 1 year period. The Family Leave is subject to the Company’s absolute discretion. It cannot be cashed out under any circumstances.

When should I take the Maternity Leave and Paternity Leave?

Maternity Leave

All full-time permanent and 1 year or above contract female employees are entitled to Maternity Leave for a continuous period of a total of 14 weeks (i.e. 98 calendar days), starting not less than 2 weeks before the expected date of confinement, in accordance with the Employment Ordinance (Cap. 57). To be eligible for full pay Maternity Leave, the employee must have completed at least 40 weeks of service before starting her maternity leave. For the purposes of work safety and protection under the relevant laws, female employees are required to submit a medical certificate of pregnancy issued by a registered medical practitioner within 12 weeks of pregnancy, or as soon as her pregnancy has been confirmed in writing by a registered medical practitioner.

Paternity Leave

All full-time permanent and 1 year or above contract male employees are entitled to 5 days of Statutory Paternity Leave on full pay for each confinement of his spouse, which can be taken consecutively or separately during the period:

  • beginning 4 weeks before the expected date of delivery of the child; and
  • ending 10 weeks from the actual date of delivery of the child.

To be eligible for full pay Statutory Paternity Leave, the employee must have passed his probation period where applicable or have worked for the Company for at least 3 months in the absence of a probation period. The application for Maternity Leave and Statutory Paternity Leave must be supported by a notice of pregnancy in accordance with the Employment Ordinance (Cap. 57). The notice must be sent to the People and Development Department when applying for the said leave.

Adoption

When should I take the Family Leave?

In the case of adoption, full-time permanent employees after 1 year of service with the Company, excluding those who are serving their notice period, are entitled to 20 days of Family Leave on full pay per adoption. A copy of birth certificate or any official documents showing the relationship between employee and the child should be submitted to the People and Development Department. The Family Leave can be taken consecutively or as a block of at least 5 work-days each time and has to be taken within 1 year of the child’s birth or adoption. Any untaken day(s) will be forfeited after the 1-year period.

When can I enroll and make the benefit changes?

Please also provide a copy of birth certificate for verification. After that, you will receive the email from CXA for benefit selection, please complete the selection and checked out the process by the deadline (around 2 – 3 weeks). Manulife will issue the medical card for your dependent, it will take around 2 weeks.

How can I get the adoption assistance?

The Adoption Assistance is provided for qualifying adoption expenses up to USD10,000 per adoption with a limit of USD20,000 per employee during employment period. What happens when my child (or children) turns aged 19 or 25?

According to our insurance policy, only full time student will be covered if the child has reached 19 years old but under 25 years old as of April 1st every year (the start date of the policy year).APAC Compensation and Benefits team will reach out to you to request for a full time student proof at the end of the policy year (around February or March each year) when your child (or children) has / have reached 19 as of April 1. In order to continue the medical insurance coverage or to be entitled to the Flex$ equivalent to the medical benefits of the child (or children) for the next policy year, please provide full time student proof (e.g. a valid student card, school fees receipt etc.) upon request. When you child (or children) has reached 25 years old as of April 1, 2020. The group medical benefits (or equivalent Flex$) will cease automatically
