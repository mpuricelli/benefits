All full-time male employees are entitled to 15 days of family leave on full pay for each confinement of their partner (provided they have been with the Company for a minimum of one year and are not serving their notice period).
Eligible employees must inform their manager in advance of their intention of taking family leave. Family leave can be taken in addition to statutory paternity leave (see the following question for more details).
Additionally, a copy of the birth certificate or any official document attesting to the relationship between the employee and the child should be submitted to the P&D Department.
Family leave can be taken consecutively or as blocks of at least five working days each and within one year of the child's birth or adoption.
Any untaken day(s) will be forfeited after the one-year period. Family leave is also subject to the Company's absolute discretion and cannot be cashed out under any circumstances.

Maternity leave

All full-time female employees with permanent or temporary contracts lasting a minimum of one year are entitled to maternity leave for a continuous period of fourteen weeks (i.e., 98 calendar days). To be eligible for maternity leave at full pay, the employee must have completed at least forty weeks of service before starting her maternity leave.
Maternity leave must be taken no less than two weeks before the expected date of birth, per the Employment Ordinance (Cap. 57).
For work safety and protection under the relevant laws, female employees must submit a medical certificate of pregnancy issued by a registered medical practitioner within twelve weeks of gestation or as soon as their pregnancy has been confirmed in writing by a registered medical practitioner.


Paternity leave

All full-time male employees with permanent or temporary contracts lasting a minimum of one year are entitled to five days of statutory paternity leave at full pay for each confinement of their partner. Paternity leave can be taken consecutively or separately during the period:

  • Beginning four weeks before the expected date of delivery of the child; and
  • Ten weeks from the actual date of delivery of the child at the latest.

To be eligible for full pay Statutory Paternity Leave, the employee must have passed his probation period when applicable or have worked for the Company for at least three months in the absence of a probation period.
The application for Maternity Leave and Statutory Paternity Leave must be supported by a notice of pregnancy in accordance with the Employment Ordinance (Cap. 57). The notice must be sent to the People and Development Department when applying for the said leave.

All full-time permanent employees with a minimum of 1 year of service with the Company (excluding those who are serving their notice period) are entitled to 20 days of family leave at full pay to care for an immediate family member (spouse, child, parent or parent-in-law) who has a serious illness.
The Family Leave can be taken consecutively or intermittently and can only be granted once in a 1-year look-back period.
When taking family leave to care for a sick relative, the employee must provide the relevant medical certification to justify the leave. The certification shall include the date at which the patient's illness started, the probable duration of the illness, the necessary duration of the employee's leave, the estimated amount of time care will be needed, and a confirmation that the patient's illness or condition warrants the employee's involvement.
Eligible employees must inform their manager of their intention of taking family leave as soon as possible.
Family leave is subject to the Company's absolute discretion and cannot be cashed out under any circumstances.
For more details, please contact the P&D Department.

Condolence Wreath

Permanent employees working full-time or with a temporary contract of a minimum of 1 year are entitled to a condolence wreath in the case of the death of an immediate family member (parents, spouse, child, parent-in-law, brother, sister, and grandparent).
At the Business/Department manager's discretion, a condolence wreath of HKD 1,000 and a token of the same value may be sent in the event of the death of an employee's immediate family member. The original receipts of the purchase should be sent to the P&D Department for reimbursement. The employee shall provide the P&D Department with the information for the arrangement of the token.

When do my benefits end?

Your employee benefits in Hong Kong will end one day after your last contractual working day at Ralph Lauren. Your P&D Business Partner will co-ordinate with the C&B team to arrange your final payment and terminate your benefit accounts.

Withdrawing or terminating my MPF (pension) account

If you wish to withdraw the balance of your MPF account, you will need to make a 'Statutory Declaration for Claim for Payment on the Grounds of Permanent Departure from Hong Kong.' Please contact your P&D Business Partner for support.

Do I need to settle all my tax liabilities before I leave Hong Kong?

The Company will notify the Inland Revenue Department (IRD) about your departure from Hong Kong. The IRD will issue a tax form for you to complete and return. Please return the tax form by the given due date.
The Immigration Department and IRD allow individuals to submit the tax return or pay the tax liabilities after departure from Hong Kong as long as they are settled by the payment due date. You may need to maintain your Hong Kong bank account open for the ease of the online tax payment or refund if you plan to do so after your departure.
The Company is under the legal obligation to withhold your final payment until the letter of release from the IRD, which confirms that you have fully settled any tax liabilities.
